11 January 1711:
At the time, Julianna Rákóczi's share of the estate (i.e. only the parts of the mansion owned by her) in
Borsi/Borša was registered. The inventory list also shows that the mansion had at least two external
wooden staircases, one of which may have been a spiral staircase. Most of the rooms were roofed and
made usable; however, the eastern side was in ruins.

 

We, the undersigned Judge and Senate of Borsi, hereby present the half of the heritage
of our Lady, the Graceful Princess Julianna Rákoczÿ, to the successor court master,
noble János Jászai Kádas.

There is a temple with a corrupted vaulted ceiling and good shingles, a thousand good
new barrels, then approximately 100 new barrels and a small tub made of pinewood.
In front of the temple, there is a chamber without a vaulted ceiling.

In the second vaulted room, closer to the banks of Bodrog river, there are two pairs
of boots sent from Makovica, a small barrel of moldy curd, then another tub half-filled
with moldy curd and a measuring vessel made of pinewood

To the south of the mansion, there is a grocery garden with good hedges,
except for the one next to the barn.

Five meadows belong to the farm; one of these is the Meadow Garden,
the lower part of this belongs to her Grace, the Princess;
