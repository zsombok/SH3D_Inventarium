About inventory lists:
Inventory lists are considered to be some of the
best sources of information, bearing testimony of the
life of early modern castles and mansions - usually,
these were recorded in writing by officials in connection
with some legal event and then placed in the archives
of the building. The purpose of the inventory lists was
to determine the contemporary value of the building,
so the condition of the doors and windows, the floors
and walls, as well as its furnishings were taken into account.

Personal items (including books, clothes, jewellery, letters)
do not appear in these lists; however, the well, the linden
tree in the yard, window frames in need of repair, and the
prisoners kept in the dungeon do.
