8 February 1694:
The most extensive state of the mansion is depicted in this inventory list, as the chapel was also listed among
its 70 rooms. However, at the time, most of them had been standing without roofs and windows for 16 years.
Of the 15 rooms that were usable, only eight had some kind of furnishings; two of these turned out to have
been recently renovated at the time. There were only two residential rooms back then.

 

Next to this guardhouse, there is a small prison cell with a vaulted ceiling,
its doors are held with forged iron bars, hung on iron hinges.
Next to the prison cell, there is a stone staircase leading up
to the upper halls, albeit in a desolate condition.

The eighth room has an attic and a simple door with iron hinges;
in it, there is a fireplace and a larder with a door.

Again, a room, formerly called the white-loaf-bakery, with a vaulted ceiling
and a door with iron hinges.

Beyond the mansion there is a mill, in which two stones
are undershot, two are overshot; along with its wheels.
The undershot stones have a new stone base, it just has
to be registered.

There are four hirelings, i.e.: Mihály Rozgoni,
Gergely Béres, János Lengyel and János Oláh.

In Bánóc (Bánovce nad Ondavou, SK) there is barley from the farm,
measured in Cassovian cubic fathoms      15 pcs
