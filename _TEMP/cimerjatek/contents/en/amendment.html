The coat of arms displayed in the amendment deed, destroyed in 1956

King Rudolf donated an amendment to the ancient coat of arms to Zsigmond Rákóczi on 27 May 1597. From this point on, the "princely" branch, members of which were barons and later even counts, used this version.

The description of the amended coat of arms and the meaning of its elements, based on the explanation stated in the donation deed, is as follows:

ROCK: symbolizes solid virtue and loyalty of the donee, defying human weakness and turns of luck
BROKEN WHEEL: turns of luck; punitive power
RED: a blood sacrifice that the owner of the coat of arms is willing to make; courage
GOLDEN: respect; power; loyalty to the king
EAGLE: it symbolizes belonging to the Holy Roman Empire and spiritual greatness,
as well as respect to the homeland and to the king
A CROWNED EAGLE: glorification of merits
