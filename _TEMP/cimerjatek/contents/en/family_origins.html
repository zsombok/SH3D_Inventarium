The village of Rákócz on the map of Zemplén county, made by Ferenc Karacs in 1804.

The Bogát-Radván clan used to live in the valley of Ondava river and the Hegyalja region before the Mongol invasion; they received their estates by royal donation. They owned several estates including the village of Rákóc (Rakovec, SK), after which they were named.

The Rákóczi, Monoky, Körtvélyessy, Hosszúmezei, Lukács, Morvay, Osvald, Posai, Bekecs and Isépy families have a common origin, while the Rákóczi branch originates from count Pál (son of Gyapoy), who lived in the late 1200s.
