The description and meaning of the coat of arms can be found in a work dedicated to and supported by György I Rákóczi, set in contemporary wording:

It is all due that the RÁKÓCZI clan received a respectable coat of arms
From his Majesty, the King, for valiant speed and advice he gave in war,
In this, a bold eagle spreads its wings, holding a sword in its right,
And so, there is its left foot too, on a wheel and holding tight,
It bears a crown and, in the centre, there, a crowned helmet shines;
which is a sign of the great faith of this respectable clan.
For the eagle's spread-out wings, swiftness and valour do proudly signify,
with the naked sabre, held in the talon of the bird, all this coincides,
And in the end, the turns of luck, peace do also bring,
The King's grace, his great promise, is shown by all these things.
